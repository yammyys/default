Executive Summary

This case study is to analyze the potential take over of Ben & Jerry’s. Ben & Jerry’s is a corporation founded by Ben Cohen and Jerry Greenfield out of a Gas Station in Vermont in 1978 in Vermont. The company is known for its community oriented behaviors and mission, and its homemade high quality but affordable ice cream that virtually anyone can afford. The company is an industry leader with sales in the 237 million dollar range. The company has a three-pronged mission dedicated to prioritizing quality of product, economic affordability, and social responsibility. The business has a dual class stock structure with different voting rights depending on the class. The supportive state law also allows for considerations of non-financial factors in acquisition decisions.

Once the company started facing declining profits, takeover was on the table. The company had started receiving takeover offers from companies like Unilever. The board was divided on how to proceed.The Co-founders Ben and Jerry wanted to continue doing what was best for the community but the board wanted to do what was best for the shareholders. Four potential bidders had offers on the table, Unilever, Dreyer’s, Meadowbrook Lane Capital, and Chartwell Investments each with advantages and drawbacks. The case evaluates against the companies financial performance and social mission and each takeover offer.
